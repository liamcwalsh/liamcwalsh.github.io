Monte Carlo + Binary Tables

As a senior seminar, I took a course focused on advanced Monte Carlo simulation methods, and the various problems these methods can solve. In particular, I looked at using a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) method to count the number of binary tables whose rows and columns summed to given constraints. I used MATLAB to perform the necessary simulations. My final writeup, which includes a more rigorous foundation of the initial problem, and the methods I used, can be found here

Empirical Analysis of Verbatim Coding

In the summer after my sophomore year (2015), I had an internship at Protobi, which is a startup based in Cambridge, MA. Protobi is a software which allows users to analyze and explore survey data in real time in the browser. As an intern, I took part in many things, including working on research projects. One of these projects looked at Protobi's verbatim coding widget, which allows users to categorize the responses to open ended survey questions. We were interested in seeing how many survey responses should you look through to be sure you have nearly all of the response categories, among other things. For more information, check out Protobi's blog

Discrete Choice Model Optimization

Another large project I undertook while at Protobi was analyzing pricing models. I looked at a logistic model, and found the optimal price for the model analytically. For more information, check out my writeup of the problem, or the writeup on Protobi
